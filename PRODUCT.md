# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Film and video production crews: directors of photography, equipment managers, camera assistants, student producers, and production coordinators. They use this on set or in prep — fast, often distracted, under pressure. They need gear accountability without friction.

## Product Purpose

CrewCase is an iOS app that tracks every piece of gear a crew checks out. It scans QR codes, logs borrowers, and alerts when gear goes overdue. It exists to eliminate the chaos of manual gear logs and the anxiety of missing equipment. Success means a crew ends every shoot with every item accounted for.

## Brand Personality

Professional, reliable, precise, quick. The brand feels like it was built by someone who has actually been on a shoot — not a SaaS product manager. It earns trust through specificity, not polish.

## References

- **Sidus Link** — precise, dark, technical UI built for cinema professionals. No decorative fluff; every element earns its place.
- **Sidus Audio** — same professional-tool register: dense information, confident typography, purpose-built feel.
- **Cadrage** — director's viewfinder app; premium craft without consumer-app sweetness. Serious, focused, tactile.

## Anti-references

- Generic productivity SaaS (Notion, Linear aesthetic) — too airy, too neutral, too "anyone"
- Consumer lifestyle apps — not bubbly, not casual, this is a work tool
- Enterprise software — not corporate, not cold, not clunky

## Design Principles

1. **Built for the set, not the boardroom.** Every word, layout, and interaction should feel like it came from someone who knows what a call sheet is.
2. **Show the pain, then show the fix.** The landing page earns the CTA by making the problem visceral before offering the solution.
3. **Speed reads as trust.** Fast load, fast scan, no friction. Slow = unreliable on set.
4. **Specificity over claims.** Name real workflows (check-out, QR scan, overdue alert) rather than vague promises. Film crews are skeptical of marketing speak.
5. **Dark is the default.** Film sets are dark. Monitors are dark. This tool lives in that world.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- No explicit WCAG level required; follow WCAG 2.1 AA as baseline
- `prefers-reduced-motion` support already in codebase — maintain it
- Color is used for status indicators (orange/green/red/purple) — ensure status is never conveyed by color alone
